Variational Network with Wavelet-based UNET in Accelerated MRI Reconstruction from Under Sampled K-space Data

Yasir Arafat Prodhan, Dr. Shaikh Anowarul Fattah

Under-grad Thesis, 2024-2025

This research presents a novel MRI reconstruction method that integrates a Wavelet-based CNN with a Variational Network (VarNet) to accelerate MRI scans using under-sampled k-space data. By leveraging multi-scale wavelet transforms and iterative reconstruction, our model improves image quality and reduces noise, outperforming traditional methods and state-of-the-art techniques. Tested on fastMRI and M4Raw datasets, the model consistently delivers higher SSIM and PSNR scores, demonstrating its potential for faster, higher-quality MRI imaging in clinical applications.

Detection and Classification of Retinopathy of Prematurity on Multi-Source Fundus Images Using DenseROPNet

Sadad Hasan, Yasir Arafat Prodhan, Dr. Mohammad Imamul Hassan Bhuiyan

2025 IEEE International Biomedical Instrumentation and Technology Conference (IBITeC), 2025

Developed DenseROPNet, a novel CNN architecture with attention mechanisms achieving 99.13% accuracy in detecting and classifying Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP) stages from fundus images. The model was trained on two diverse datasets from different ethnic populations to ensure robust generalization across healthcare settings. Incorporated Explainable AI techniques to validate that the model focuses on clinically relevant anatomical features, making it suitable as an effective screening tool for resource-limited neonatal units.

Skin Cancer Semantic Segmentation

Anindha Dhar, Diganta Sikdar, Yasir Arafat Prodhan, Sourov Joardar

IEEE International Conference on Electrical, Computer and Communication Engineering (ECCE), 13-15 Feb, 2025

This study compares CNN-based (UNET) and hybrid CNN-transformer (TransUNET) architectures for skin cancer lesion segmentation using the ISIC-2018 dataset. The research demonstrates that TransUNET outperforms traditional UNET, achieving a Dice score of 0.91183, while polar coordinate transformation of images further improves segmentation performance to 0.935 by reducing decision boundary complexity. The findings show that combining transformer-based global feature extraction with CNNs and polar image preprocessing significantly enhances medical image segmentation accuracy.
